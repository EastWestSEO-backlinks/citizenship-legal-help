Why U.S. Citizenship Matters

Understanding the Legal Requirements for U.S. Citizenship

To qualify for U.S. citizenship through the naturalization process, applicants must meet the following criteria:

    1. Lawful Permanent Resident Status

    You must hold a green card and have been a lawful permanent resident (LPR) for at least five years (or three years if married to a U.S. citizen).

    2. Continuous Residency

    You must have continuously lived in the U.S. for the required period of time and demonstrate physical presence in the country for a certain number of days.

    3. Good Moral Character

    Applicants must demonstrate good moral character, which includes factors like paying taxes and avoiding serious criminal activity.

    4. English Language Proficiency

    Most applicants are required to have basic proficiency in the English language, both in speaking and writing.

    5. U.S. Civics Knowledge

    You must pass a civics test that covers important aspects of U.S. history, government, and the Constitution.

    6. Oath of Allegiance

    After approval, you will take the Oath of Allegiance to the U.S., formally completing the naturalization process.
